Chapter 1: Electric Current

Electric Current

Electric current is the flow of electric charge through a conductor. In most circuits the moving charges are electrons.

The unit of electric current is the Ampere (A).

Electric Circuit

An electric circuit is a closed path that allows electric current to flow.

  • Battery
  • Wire
  • Load (lamp)
  • Switch

Voltage

Voltage is the potential difference that pushes electric charges through a circuit.

Unit: Volt (V)

Resistance

Resistance is the opposition to the flow of current.

Unit: Ohm (Ω)

Ohm's Law

Ohm's law shows the relationship between voltage, current and resistance.

V = I × R
