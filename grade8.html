Wave Behavior Concepts

1. Reflection

Reflection happens when a wave bounces off a surface.

Law of Reflection:

  • Incident angle (θi) = Reflection angle (θr)
  • Angles are measured from the normal line

Reflection occurs in mirrors:

  • Convex mirrors – used in vehicle mirrors because they give a wider view
  • Concave mirrors – used in flashlights and dentist mirrors because they focus light

2. Refraction

Refraction is the bending of light when it passes from one medium to another (for example air to water).

  • Less dense → more dense medium → bends toward the normal
  • More dense → less dense medium → bends away from the normal

This happens because light changes speed in different materials.

3. Refractive Index

The refractive index (n) measures how much a material slows down light.

Speed of light in vacuum ≈ 300,000,000 m/s

Example: Acrylic has n = 1.49

This means light travels 1.49 times slower in acrylic than in vacuum.

4. Snell's Law

Snell's Law explains how light bends when it moves between materials.

Formula:

n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂

  • n₁ , n₂ = refractive index
  • θ₁ = incident angle
  • θ₂ = refracted angle

5. Total Internal Reflection

Total internal reflection happens when light cannot leave a medium and reflects completely inside it.

Example:

  • Optical fibers
  • Internet communication cables

6. Interference

Interference occurs when two or more waves overlap.

  • Constructive interference → waves combine and become stronger
  • Destructive interference → waves cancel each other

7. Diffraction

Diffraction is the spreading of waves when they pass through a small opening or around an obstacle.

Examples:

  • Laser light through a slit
  • Water waves in ripple tanks
