Definition of Measurement

Measurement is the act of finding the size, amount, or value of something by comparing it with a standard unit. Standard units help people measure things in the same way so results are clear and accurate.

For example, when measuring the length of a table, we use units such as meters or centimeters and compare the table's length to those units using a ruler or measuring tape.

Types of Physical Quantities

Length

Length refers to the distance between two points or how long an object is. The main unit in the metric system is the meter (m).

  • Kilometer (km) – long distances
  • Centimeter (cm) – small objects
  • Millimeter (mm) – very small measurements
  • Micrometer (μm) – microscopic objects
  • Nanometer (nm) – extremely tiny particles

Length Units

  • 1 km = 1000 m
  • 1 cm = 0.01 m
  • 1 mm = 0.001 m
  • 1 μm = 0.000001 m
  • 1 nm = 0.000000001 m

Temperature

Temperature shows how hot or cold something is. It is an important quantity used in science and everyday life.

  • Celsius (°C) – commonly used
  • Fahrenheit (°F) – used in the United States
  • Kelvin (K) – SI unit used in science

Length Conversion Examples

Example 1

Convert 3 kilometers to meters

1 km = 1000 m

3 km = 3 × 1000

= 3000 m

Example 2

Convert 75 cm to meters

1 cm = 0.01 m

75 × 0.01

= 0.75 m

Example 3

Convert 4500 mm to meters

1 mm = 0.001 m

4500 × 0.001

= 4.5 m

Temperature Conversion Examples

Celsius to Kelvin

Formula:

K = °C + 273

Example:

25°C + 273

= 298 K

Celsius to Fahrenheit

Formula:

°F = (9/5 × °C) + 32

Example:

(9/5 × 30) + 32

= 86°F
